## 1. What is the Extended ER (EER) Model?

The **Extended ER (EER) Model** is an improvement over the basic ER model.

The basic ER model supports:

* Entities
* Attributes
* Relationships

But **realâ€‘world systems are more complex**. The EER model adds advanced concepts to represent:

* Different types of the same entity
* Hierarchical relationships
* Relationships between relationships

The three most important EER concepts are:

* **Specialization**
* **Generalization**
* **Aggregation**

## 2. Specialization (In Depth)

### 2.1 What is Specialization?

**Specialization** is the process of **dividing one general entity into multiple specialized entities**.

In simple words:

> We start with a **common entity** and break it into **more specific entities**.

It follows a **Topâ€‘Down approach**.

### 2.5 Constraints in Specialization

#### A. Disjoint Constraint

* One entity can belong to **only one** subclass

Example:

> An employee can be **either Manager OR Intern**, not both

#### B. Overlapping Constraint

* One entity can belong to **multiple** subclasses

Example:

> A person can be both **Teacher AND Researcher**

---

### 2.6 Completeness Constraint

| Type                       | Meaning                                      |
| -------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **Total Specialization**   | Every entity must be in some subclass        |
| **Partial Specialization** | Some entities may not belong to any subclass |

Example:

* Total: Every employee must be Manager, Engineer, or Intern
* Partial: Some employees may not fall into any category

## 5. Aggregation (In Depth)

### 5.1 What is Aggregation?

**Aggregation** is used when:

> A **relationship itself needs to participate in another relationship**.

In simple words:

> We treat a **relationship as an entity**.
